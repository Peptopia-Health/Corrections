COWS Study Guide

Clinical Opiate Withdrawal Scale Assessment

Overview

The Clinical Opiate Withdrawal Scale (COWS) is an 11-item clinical tool used by healthcare providers to measure the severity of opioid withdrawal and track a patient's progress during detoxification. The assessment scores both subjective symptoms and objective physical signs.

The 11 Assessed Symptoms

For each item, the assessor rates the severity on a scale, based strictly on symptoms related to opiate withdrawal rather than other conditions:

  • Resting Pulse Rate: Counted in beats per minute after the patient sits or rests for one minute.
  • Sweating: Observed over the past 30 minutes (not related to room temperature).
  • Restlessness: Observed as the patient attempts to sit still.
  • Pupil Size: Evaluated in normal room light.
  • Bone or Joint Aches: Specifically attributed to opiate withdrawal.
  • Runny Nose or Tearing Eyes: Not caused by a cold or allergies.
  • GI Upset: Stomach cramps, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea over the last 30 minutes.
  • Tremor: Shaking of hands or muscles, as observed by the assessor.
  • Yawning: Frequency of yawning observed during the assessment.
  • Anxiety or Irritability: The patient's emotional state during the assessment.
  • Gooseflesh Skin: Piloerection observed or felt on the arms.

Scoring Interpretation

The points from all 11 categories are tallied to determine the severity:

Mild5 to 12 points
Moderate13 to 24 points
Moderately Severe25 to 36 points
SevereMore than 36 points

Clinical Use

The COWS tool is frequently used to determine if a patient is in an optimal window of withdrawal to safely begin medication treatments like buprenorphine (such as Suboxone) to prevent precipitated withdrawal.

To view or print the exact scoring sheet, you can reference the National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA) Clinical Opiate Withdrawal Scale or the ASAM COWS Induction Flow Sheet.
