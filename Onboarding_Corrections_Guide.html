Section I: Workplace Ethics & Conduct

⚖️ Conduct Standards

  • Intent vs. Impact: Harassment is defined by the impact on the victim, not the intent of the actor. "I was just kidding" is not a valid legal defense.
  • Incivility: Low-intensity deviant behavior (rudeness, discourtesy) with ambiguous intent to harm.
  • Hostile Work Environment: Pervasive conduct severe enough to alter terms of employment.
  • Quid Pro Quo: "This for that"—sexual favors exchanged for employment benefits.

🚨 Violence in Healthcare

75% of all workplace assaults occur in healthcare settings.

Warning Signs: Social withdrawal, deteriorating hygiene, fascination with weapons, and unexplained absenteeism.

Section VI: Clinical Screening Protocols

👁️ Ocular Assessment

  • Sequence: Right (OD), Left (OS), then Both (OU).
  • Scoring: Record misses (e.g., 20/50 -2). If >2 letters missed, move up one line.
  • Normal: Sclera white, Conjunctiva pink/moist, Pupils PERRL.
  • Anatomy: Iris = Color; Pupil = Dark center.

🦷 Oral & Nose Inspection

  • Tonsils: Should NOT touch each other. Touching = abnormal.
  • Tongue: Should be pink. White coating is pathologic.
  • Nose: Internal check for drainage (color/odor) and obstructions.
  • Nose Baseline: No difficulty passing air through nares is the normal finding.

🍏 Abdominal Assessment

  • Order: Inspection $\rightarrow$ Auscultation $\rightarrow$ Palpation $\rightarrow$ Percussion.
  • Sounds: Must occur every 5 – 10 seconds.
  • Findings: Abdomen should be soft and non-distended.
  • Watch for: Rebound pain (palpation) or hernias (inspection).

👤 Neck & Lymphatic

  • Baseline: No visible veins or arteries.
  • Palpation: Carotid pulses must be palpable bilaterally. Feel for goiter or lymph node enlargement under jaw.
  • Mobility: Full ROM without pain or restriction is required.

🫁 Respiratory Evaluation

  • Signs: Unlabored effort, equal chest expansion, no deformities.
  • Abnormal: Dry cough or productive cough color must be noted.
  • Auscultation: Clear sounds in both anterior and posterior fields.

Section VII: Documentation & H&P Standards

✍️ Legal Medical Record

❗ The History and Physical (H&P) is a mandatory component of the legal health record. If it is not documented, it legally was not done.

  • Paper forms: Must be legible.
  • Identifiers: Must include Patient Name and Inmate ID Number.
  • Timing: Must include exact Date and Time of entry.
  • Auth: Requires Full Signature AND Professional Title.

📋 Referral & Deferral

  • Referrals: A nurse can refer a patient to the provider at any time based on clinical assessment.
  • Deferrals: Portions of exams (e.g., breast/rectal) may be deferred but MUST be documented.
  • Requirement: Documentation must state what was deferred and to whom the exam was referred.

Section VIII: Environmental Safety & Occupational Risks

📉 Fall Prevention & Mechanics

  • Falls are the 3rd leading cause of unintentional injury deaths.
  • ❗ Institutional Standard: Falls are 100% preventable.
  • Trips: Caused by as little as a 3/8" rise in a walkway.
  • Slips: Front foot slip usually causes a person to fall backward.

🥋 The "Safe Fall" Technique

  • DO NOT hold out hands/elbows to break the fall (causes fractures).
  • Tuck chin to protect the head; bend all limbs.
  • Roll or twist to the side; land on side/buttocks rather than back.
  • Notify supervisor immediately after any fall.

♿ Safe Patient Handling (SPH)

The single greatest risk factor for overexertion injuries in healthcare is manual lifting/repositioning of patients.

❗ Uncooperative Patients: Use a full sling lift and TWO more caregivers (total of 3 people).

Section IX: Wound Management & Pressure Injuries

🩹 Wound Assessment

  • Nutrition: Adequate Protein and Vitamin C intake is mandatory for tissue repair.
  • Measurements: Order must be Length x Width x Depth in centimeters.
  • Drainage: Purulent (yellow/green/brown) = Clinical Infection.

🛌 Pressure Injury Staging

  • Stage I: Intact skin; non-blanchable erythema.
  • Stage II: Partial-thickness skin loss; shallow open ulcer, red-pink bed, no slough.
  • Stage III: Full-thickness tissue loss; subcutaneous fat visible; bone/muscle NOT exposed.
  • Stage IV: Full-thickness loss with exposed bone, tendon, or muscle.

⭐ MASTER CLINICAL COMMANDMENTS

🎯 Documentation is the #1 way to coordinate care among providers.

🎯 Abdominal assessment sequence: Inspection $\rightarrow$ Auscultation $\rightarrow$ Palpation $\rightarrow$ Percussion.

🎯 Normal bowel sounds occur every 5 to 10 seconds in all quadrants.

🎯 A nurse can refer a patient to the provider at any time.

🎯 Manual patient lifting is the single greatest risk for overexertion injuries.

🎯 For uncooperative patients, use a full sling lift and TWO more caregivers.

🎯 NEVER hold out your hands or elbows to break a fall; bend your limbs.

🎯 The History and Physical is a formal part of the legal health record.

🎯 Stage II pressure injury: Shallow open ulcer, red-pink wound bed, NO slough.

🎯 Stage III pressure injury: Fat may be visible; bone/tendon NOT exposed.

🎯 Stage IV pressure injury: Direct exposure of bone, tendon, or muscle.

🎯 Conduct is judged by its impact on the victim, not actor intent.
