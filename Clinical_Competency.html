Section I: Emergency Response & Trauma

  • Safety Rule: Personal safety is the absolute first priority in any response.
  • Hanging: Mandatory aggressive resuscitation for 100% of victims.
  • Emergency Position: Side-lying for unresponsive patients with pulse and breathing.

Section II: Medication Administration

🛡️ Med Pass Security & Safety

Standard Operations

  • Handwashing: Mandatory just prior to starting med pass.
  • Safest Method: Punch and Pass.
  • Med Cart Security: Must be locked at all times when unattended.
  • Approach Protocol: Only 1 patient approaches the cart at a time.

Pre-Pouring Restrictions

Allowed ONLY in: Lockdowns, inaccessible tiers, or with Board permission.

  • Administer within 60 minutes.
  • Same nurse pours, transports, and administers.
  • PROHIBITED: Never pre-pour controlled substances.

🚫 Diversion Prevention

Responsibility: Joint effort between Medical AND Security.

Mouth Checks: Drink of water before meds; inspection after meds.

Hand Checks: Spread fingers, palms up.

DOT (Direct Observation)

ALWAYS DOT:

Controlled substances, Psychotropic meds, HIV meds, and TB meds.

⭐ Must-Know Knowledge Points

  • APPROACH Only ONE (1) patient should approach the med cart at a time.
  • CART Med carts must be locked whenever unattended; never leave unlocked in med room.
  • PRE-POUR Limit: 60 minutes; Never pre-pour Controlled Substances.
  • HYGIENE Handwashing is mandatory just prior to starting med pass.
  • DOT Controlled, Psychotropic, HIV, and TB meds are ALWAYS DOT.
  • DIVERSION Requires both Mouth Checks and Hand Checks (spread fingers).
