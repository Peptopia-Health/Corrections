I. Triage & Sick Call Management
HSR process, sick call workflow, compliance rules
HSR (Health Service Request) Process

All workflows start from the selected Patient chart.

Click FORMS.
Select EXAM FORMS.
Scroll down and select the "Service Request Triage" form.
Save and Lock.
Sick Call Process
  • Click the Sick Calls tab.
  • Select New Sick Call → Nurse → Begin.
  • Open the Subjective Interview Form: select the problem/complaint, complete, Save and Lock, then close.
  • Scroll to Objective: complete clinical findings, Save and Lock.
Managing Generated Sick Call Tasks
  • A completed HSR form generates a TASK for the sick call.
  • Check the "Today" and "Overdue" tabs under TASKS.
  • Click the clock icon → select "Begin Medical" → follow subjective/objective steps.
Mandatory Compliance Rules
  • Timeframe: Sick calls MUST be completed within 48 hrs.
  • Vitals: Height & weight required on ALL sick calls alongside standard VS.
  • Paper Trail: Physical sick call paper needs FTF, date, and time by the assessing nurse.
II. Detox Medication Orders
Creating & completing detox med order tasks
Creating a Detox Med Order

Used to communicate critical info with the provider (verified meds, positive drug tests, Pt concerns).

Click on TASKS.
Select ADD TASK.
Open the drop-down and choose Detox Med Order.
Prerequisite Documentation
  • A detailed chart note or sick call must be completed FIRST before requesting a med/care plan change.
Completing a Detox Med Order
  • Click TASKS → check "Today" or "Overdue" tabs.
  • Click the clock icon for the correct task.
  • Complete the task and create a short note at the bottom.
  • Click Submit Change.
  • If receiving new orders → complete a Medical Provider Form for verbal orders.
III. Medical Provider Orders (Verbal)
Documenting verbal orders from providers

Strictly used to document verbal orders received directly from the provider.

Select the FORMS tab.
Select EXAM FORMS.
Choose the second option: "Medical Provider Order" → select Begin.
Select "No".
Select preset orders OR use box 22 to free-type specific orders.
Ensure box 23 is checked "Yes".
Enter the provider's name exactly in section 24.
Save and Lock.
IV. Medical Emergency — Incident Report
Documenting emergencies: admin data, narrative, assessment & interventions
Critical: Time-Sensitive Documentation
  • An Incident Report must be initiated immediately following any medical emergency.
  • All four sections of the report must be completed in full before the end of the shift in which the incident occurred.
Step 1 — Administrative Data

Capture identifying information immediately to timestamp the event.

Record the Date and Time of the incident.
Document the specific Location (housing unit / medical area).
Enter Patient Name (Last, First M.) and Patient ID.
Step 2 — Narrative Log

Provide a clear, factual account of what happened.

  • Initial Observation: Describe what was seen or heard immediately before or at the start of the event.
  • Sequence of Events: Document the timeline clearly — what happened first, second, etc. Include involvement of other staff members.
Narrative Best Practices
  • Use objective, factual language — avoid opinions or assumptions.
  • Write events in chronological order with specific times where possible.
  • Identify all staff involved by name and role.
Step 3 — Clinical Assessment Log

Complete a structured clinical evaluation of the patient.

Physical Exam: Document skin, head, extremities, and vitals observed.
Injuries Noted: Record presence or absence of any injuries.
Patient Statement: Document the patient's own words verbatim.
History Obtained: Record dietary, hydration, and medication history.
Assessment Reminder
  • Patient statements must be recorded word-for-word (verbatim) — do not paraphrase.
  • Document both the presence AND absence of injuries for a complete record.
Step 4 — Interventions & Actions

Record all treatments, directives, and monitoring actions taken.

  • Treatment Administered: Document all medications given, including dosage.
  • Directives Given: Record any instructions given to staff or the patient.
  • Supervision: Document how the patient was monitored following the event.
