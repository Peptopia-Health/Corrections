🧠 Mental Health Triage

Peptopia Health • Clinical Compendium

Comprehensive protocol for clinical screening, risk stratification, and behavioral health.

📋 Section I: Triage Framework & Communication

Triage is a dynamic, ongoing process used to determine clinical priority based on safety and acuity. Its primary purpose is to manage Limited Facility Resources efficiently while identifying immediate risks.

🗣️ Therapeutic Communication

Goal: Promote patient autonomy and healthy coping.
Techniques: Involves active listening, therapeutic silence, reflection of feelings, and using open-ended questions like "Tell me more about that experience."

🚫 Non-Therapeutic Barriers

Avoid giving personal advice, offering false reassurance, or asking demanding "Why" questions, as these shut down therapeutic rapport.

🛠️ C-SSRS Tool

The Columbia–Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) is an evidence-supported screening tool. A key benefit is that it requires no special mental health training for basic administration by nursing or intake staff.

📊 Section II: Risk Stratification & Defense Mechanisms

Evaluating mental health acuity requires categorizing presentation into strict risk levels to dictate operational response times.

Risk Tier Clinical Presentation Mandatory Evaluation Window
💀 High Risk Active suicidal ideation, threats of harm, or self-injury. Emergent Evaluation: Immediate specialty transfer or Suicide Watch. Immediate Priority.
🟡 Moderate Risk Active Psychosis (delusions/hallucinations), severe ADL decline, or confusion. Urgent Evaluation: QMHP assessment within 24 to 72 hours. Secondary Priority.
🟢 Low Risk New mental health complaints or minor symptom changes. Routine Evaluation: Assessment within 7 to 14 working days. Routine Priority.

🧠 Psychological Defense Mechanisms

  • Reaction Formation: Expressing the exact opposite of true unacceptable feelings.
  • Sublimation: Channeling unacceptable impulses into constructive, socially acceptable activities.
  • Projection: Attributing one's own traits or feelings to others (e.g., "The nurse hates me" when the patient is actually angry).
  • Displacement: Shifting anger or impulses to a less-threatening target.

🛡️ Section III: Clinical Operations & Safety Standards

🗓️ SCR & Clinical Encounters

  • Daily Access: All patients must have the opportunity DAILY to request mental health care via Sick Call.
  • Encounter Timeframe: If a request describes clinical symptoms, a face-to-face encounter must occur within 24 hours.
  • Documentation: Any triage of a request MUST include a review of the patient's health record. Ensure accurate SOAPE Notes (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan, Education).
  • SCR Logs: Must track the date of receipt, the date of triage (< 24h), and the date of completion.

⚖️ Safety & Restraints

  • Restraints: Used strictly as a last resort for imminent physical danger. Adult orders must be renewed every 4 hours. Monitor physical needs and circulation every 15-30 minutes.
  • Rule of Caution: Always err on the side of caution with interventions and watch levels.
  • Referrals: All emergent referrals must be CALLED to the provider immediately.
  • Conflict: If clinicians disagree on triage level: Talk it out first → then seek supervisor guidance.

💊 Section IV: Pharmacological Pearls

💀 Lithium Toxicity Risk

Therapeutic Range: 0.6 – 1.2 mEq/L.

Toxicity Signs: Coarse tremors, ataxia, slurred speech, and severe diarrhea. Monitor closely for dehydration, which increases toxicity risk.

💀 Serotonin Syndrome

Hallmark Signs: Hyperreflexia, high fever, tachycardia, and rapid mental status changes.

Standard Intervention: Discontinue all serotonergic agents immediately and notify the provider.
