CIWA Guide

Clinical Institute Withdrawal Assessment

Overview

The core difference in the Clinical Institute Withdrawal Assessment (CIWA) tools lies in the substance being monitored: CIWA-Ar measures alcohol withdrawal, while CIWA-B evaluates benzodiazepine withdrawal. Both are clinical scoring tools used by medical professionals to track symptom severity and guide medication dosing, but they differ significantly in their format and focus.

Key Differences at a Glance

Feature CIWA-Ar (Alcohol) CIWA-B (Benzodiazepines)
Primary Use Tracks alcohol withdrawal severity. Tracks benzodiazepine withdrawal severity.
Number of Items 10 core symptoms. Up to 22 specific items.
Scoring Range 0 to 67. 0 to 80 (or up to 140 depending on version).
Symptom Focus Sweating, tremors, nausea, tactile/visual/auditory disturbances, and orientation. Anxiety, paresthesia (tingling), sensory disturbances (like sensitivity to light/sound), depersonalization, and paranoia.

How They Differ in Clinical Practice

CIWA-Ar (Revised)

This is the gold standard for managing alcohol withdrawal in the hospital. Scores help healthcare providers determine if and how much medication (often benzodiazepines) to give.

CIWA-B

This is a much longer, specialized tool validated specifically to distinguish benzo withdrawal symptoms (like severe anxiety, sensory overload, and paranoia) from a patient's baseline symptoms. It requires more comprehensive patient input to distinguish psychological and neurological effects.
