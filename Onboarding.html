Integrated Clinical Training

I. Workplace Integrity & Ethics

⚖️ Legal Standards

  • • Hostile Environment: Conduct that is so severe or pervasive it fundamentally alters employment terms and creates an abusive working environment. 🛑
  • • Quid Pro Quo: Literally meaning "this for that." This occurs when sexual favors are requested in exchange for work benefits, promotions, or avoiding disciplinary action. 🚫
  • • ❗ Intent vs. Impact: Harassment is strictly judged by the impact on the victim, not the actor's intent. "I was just kidding" is never a valid legal defense.
  • • Incivility: Low-intensity rude, discourteous, or disrespectful behavior that disrupts workplace harmony.

🚨 Workplace Violence

Nearly 75% of all workplace assaults nationwide occur in healthcare settings. 🏥

Watch for these critical behavioral indicators:

  • Deteriorating hygiene or physical appearance.
  • An unhealthy fascination with weapons or military talk.
  • Sudden absenteeism or extreme social withdrawal.
  • Erratic, unpredictable mood swings.

II. PREA & Vulnerable Populations

• Screening Timeline: An initial vulnerability screen is legally required within 72 hours of a patient's arrival. ⏱️

• Historical Referrals: If an inmate discloses historical abuse (occurring >3 months ago), a Mental Health referral must be placed within 14 days.

• ❗ High-Risk Vulnerability: Mentally ill inmates are statistically 8 times more likely to be victims of abuse within a facility. 🧠

• Youth Separation: Sight and sound separation is mandatory for inmates under the age of 18 from the adult population.

🕵️ Evidence Preservation

Biological evidence degrades quickly. Patients must NOT engage in the following until evidence is secured:

  • ❌ Eating or Drinking
  • ❌ Smoking
  • ❌ Showering or Bathing
  • ❌ Brushing Teeth
  • ❌ Urinating/Defecating
  • ❌ Changing Clothes

III. Physical Assessment Baselines

👁️ Ocular (Snellen)

  • Sequence: Evaluate OD (Right), OS (Left), then OU (Both).
  • Scoring Rule: A score of 20/50 -2 means they missed exactly 2 letters on that line. If >2 are missed, you must move UP to the previous line.
  • Anatomy: The Iris provides the color. The Pupil is the dark opening in the center.
  • Norms: Sclera should be white; Conjunctiva should be pink and moist.

🍏 Abdominal Exam

  • Procedural Order:
    Inspection → Auscultation → Palpation → Percussion.

    (Auscultate first so palpation doesn't stimulate false bowel activity).
  • Bowel Sounds: Normal clinical findings require bowel sounds to be present in all 4 quadrants every 5 – 10 seconds. ⏱️

👤 Neck & Lungs

  • Neck Baseline: No visible veins or arteries should be noted during resting inspection. Carotid pulses should be palpable bilaterally.
  • Lungs: Chest expansion must be equal. A dry cough is always considered an abnormal finding. 🫁
  • Lymphatics: Feel for enlargement along the jawline and neck base.

💪 Extremity Assessment

  • Assess for Edema, Motor Strength, Movement (ROM), Presence of prosthetics, and Pulses.
  • Bilateral radial and pedal pulses must be equal and strong.

📋 Vitals & Oral

  • ❗ Stated weight is NOT a vital sign. Staff must physically measure it.
  • Skin tenting indicates dehydration.
  • Oral: Tonsils should NOT touch. A white coating on the tongue is pathologic (Thrush). 👅

IV. Medication Management

💊 Admin Standards

  • • Pre-Pouring: Hard limit of 60 minutes. Controlled meds are never allowed to be pre-poured.
  • • Pre-Pour Labels: Must have Patient Name, ID, Med Name, Dose, Date/Time, and Special Instructions.
  • • Hand Hygiene: Handwashing is strictly required just prior to beginning the med pass. 🧼
  • • MAR Documentation: Circle initials on the front if missed; write out the full explanation on the **BACK** (no shorthand codes on front).

🩸 Insulin Kinetics & Injection Rules

  • • Regular Insulin: Onset 30-60m | Peak 2-4 hours.
  • • Rapid Acting: Onset 15m | Peak 30-90m.
  • ❗ NO MIXING RULES:
    70/30 Insulin is NEVER mixed with Regular. If coverage is needed, give a second injection.
    Lantus is NEVER mixed with any other insulin.
  • IM Volume Caps: Deltoid (2ml), Dorsogluteal (4ml), Ventral Gluteal (5ml).

V. OUD Pharmacology

• Methadone: Full agonist. Requires EKG monitoring due to the risk of life-threatening QT Prolongation. 📉

• Buprenorphine: Partial agonist. Has a Ceiling Effect on respiratory depression, making it safer for overdose risk.

• Suboxone: Combination of Buprenorphine and Naloxone.

• Naltrexone: Antagonist (no opioid effect). Vivitrol is the extended-release injectable form.

🔄 Diversion Control

While diversion is a reality in corrections, nursing response must remain non-judgmental. Management relies on Directly Observed Therapy (DOT), mouth checks, and random Urine Drug Screens (UDS).

VI. Public Health & Herbals

🦠 Clinical Markers

  • • Trichomoniasis: Characterized by a "Strawberry Cervix" (petechiae) and frothy discharge.
  • • Secondary Syphilis: Presents with systemic rashes, particularly on the palms of the hands and soles of the feet.

🌿 Herbal Interactions

  • • St. John's Wort: Highly interactive. Causes Serotonin Syndrome if mixed with SSRIs. Decreases the effectiveness of Digoxin.
  • • Licorice: Mimics aldosterone, leading to dangerous Hypertension and Hypokalemia.
  • • Ginkgo/Ginseng: Increases bleeding risk; must stop 2 weeks prior to surgery.

VII. Safety & Wound Care

⚠️ Fall & Lift Safety

• Fall Mechanics: Falls are the 3rd leading cause of injury death. A front-foot slip causes a backward fall. A trip can be caused by a 3/8" rise in a floor surface.

• Technique: Tuck chin, bend all limbs, and roll. NEVER use hands or elbows to brace a fall (causes fractures).

• Safe Patient Handling: Uncooperative patient lifts require a mechanical sling and TWO more caregivers (3 total staff).

🩹 Wound Staging

• Stage I: Intact skin; non-blanchable erythema (redness).

• Stage II: Partial-thickness loss; shallow open ulcer, no slough.

• Stage III: Full tissue loss; Subcutaneous fat may be visible; bone/muscle are NOT exposed.

• Stage IV: Exposed bone, tendon, or muscle.

Assessment: Always measure Length x Width x Depth (cm). Protein and Vit C are vital for healing.

Clinical Key Points

🎯 70/30 Insulin is NEVER mixed with Regular insulin.

🎯 Documentation is the #1 tool for coordination of clinical care.

🎯 Normal bowel sounds occur every 5 to 10 seconds in all quadrants.

🎯 A nurse can refer a patient to the provider at any time based on clinical judgment.

🎯 Manual patient lifting is the #1 risk factor for worker overexertion.

🎯 PREA screening must be completed within 72 hours of arrival.

🎯 Stage III pressure injuries: Fat may be visible, bone is NOT exposed.

🎯 Jointly count controlled substances at EVERY shift change and key handoff.

🎯 The person discovering a medication error MUST report it (MEAT Tool).

🎯 Tucking the chin is vital to head protection during a fall.

🎯 Regular Insulin peak action is 2 to 4 hours.

🎯 Stated weight is NOT a vital sign; staff measurement is mandatory.

🎯 Trichomoniasis presents with a Strawberry Cervix.

🎯 Blanket medication refusals are prohibited; must sign for each dose.
